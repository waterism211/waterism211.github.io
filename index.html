About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Statistics at the University of Maryland, specializing in variational Bayesian methods, amortized inference, robustness, and generative AI. My work integrates theory and scalable computation for high-dimensional latent-variable models, spanning Bayesian nonparametrics, dynamical systems, and simulation-based inference.

Selected Research

Variational Bagging

Robust ensemble correction for mean-field VI, improving calibration, predictive coverage, and stability.

Structured Amortized VI

Time-dependent variational families with theoretical risk bounds and improved posterior accuracy.

Simulation-Based VI

Likelihood-free approximate inference with forward-KL objectives and statistical guarantees.

Krylov Subspace Methods

Implemented GMRES and analyzed convergence under conditioning and preconditioning strategies.
