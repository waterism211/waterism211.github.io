About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Statistics at the University of Maryland, College Park. My research focuses on Bayesian statistics, variational inference, generative AI, and scalable methods for high-dimensional latent-variable models. I work on theoretical and practical aspects of variational Bayes, including amortized inference, robust aggregation, likelihood-free inference, and uncertainty quantification.

Selected Research Projects

  • Variational Bagging: Developed an ensemble-style correction for mean-field VI improving covariance estimation, robustness, and predictive uncertainty.
  • Robust and Scalable Variational Bayes: Introduced geometric-median aggregated variational posteriors with strong theoretical guarantees and robustness.
  • Amortized Structured Variational Inference: Designed time-dependent structured VI families, derived risk bounds, and demonstrated predictive gains in nonlinear dynamical systems.
  • Simulation-Based Variational Inference: Studied forward-KL objectives for likelihood-free models and established theoretical guarantees.
  • Krylov Subspace Methods: Implemented GMRES algorithms for large sparse linear systems and analyzed convergence under conditioning and preconditioning.
